The Missing Piece

The problem is that IV drug users usually fall through the cracks of the healthcare system. They rarely see doctors and if they do most will not admit to being an IV drug user, a problem because 63% have Hepatitis C and 15% have HIV. IV drug users end up costing hundreds of millions of dollars in preventable healthcare costs every year because they are not being treated.

Our system profiles, recruits, engages, and supports IV drug users through existing harm reduction innitiatives. Once registered we coordinate screening and doctors' visits. We apply state of the art preventative healthcare interventions as games; rewarding incentives for doing healthy behaviours. This includes being screened, seeing the doctor, learning about their diseases, taking prescribed medication, etc.

In the end, highly vulnerable patients are receiving care, chich eases costs on the healthcare system, pharmacies generate a new revenue flow, and pharma's sales increase. All stakeholders win.

We are leveraging existing patient behaviour, established infrastructure, and things that are happening anyway. We are not trying to re-invent the wheel, instead we bring value to all stakeholders who participate in exchange for using a small piece of their wheel.
